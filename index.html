Why these placements matter

Peripheral nociceptor

Histamine and inflammatory cytokines sensitize nociceptor endings in injured skin, lowering threshold and amplifying input into the dorsal horn.

Dorsal horn excitation

Primary afferent terminals in superficial dorsal horn release glutamate for fast excitation and substance P during stronger or sustained nociceptive input.

Spinal inhibition

A-beta input can engage inhibitory interneurons. GABA, glycine, and enkephalin reduce transmitter release and dampen transmission-neuron firing.

Descending control

RVM serotonergic input can inhibit or facilitate dorsal horn signaling. LC noradrenergic input is classically inhibitory through spinal alpha-2 mechanisms.
